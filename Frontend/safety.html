Safety & Prevention Tips

  • 1. Never talk to unknown person over internet.
  • 2. Never click on unknown link as it may harm you in many ways.
  • 3. Ignore fake emails containing attracting contents(like lucky draw winner, free vouchers) as it be scam.
  • 4. Apart from its primary role, Nepal Police conducts a vast array of duties such as VIP protection, Security of Vital Installations, Traffic management, Secret services, Intelligence collection, Riot Control, Disaster management, Hostage rescue and various other Ceremonial roles.
  • 5. Be aware that your mobile device is vulnerable to viruses and hackers. Download applications from trusted sources.

  • 1. You are your own best protection.
  • 2. Keep your door locked at all times. Don’t unlock the door for strangers. Keep first-floor windows locked.
  • 3. Walk in well-lit areas and stay near the curb, away from alleys, entry ways and bushes where someone could be hiding.
  • 4. Do not walk or jog alone after dark.
  • 5. Avoid short cuts. Walk where there are other people present.

  • 1. Try to avoid isolated bus or train stops and times when few people are around.
  • 2. Stay away from the curb until bus/train arrives.
  • 3. Have the exact fare or ticket/pass ready as you board the bus.
  • 4. If possible, sit near the driver and notify him or her of any problems.
  • 5. A crowded bus is a prime target for pick pockets; carry your bags close and carry your wallet in your coat or front pant pocket.

  • 1. Never give personal information to people that you don’t know (name, home address, phone number, etc.).
  • 2. If you decide to talk to someone on the phone don’t give out your number; call them and use caller ID block.
  • 3. Computers, especially if they are portable, are primary targets of theft. Consider the purchase of a locking security or tracking device.
  • 4. Contact Public Safety to borrow engravers; engrave computers, stereos, and televisions with your driver's license number (including home state) or department name. Do not engrave on removable serial number plates.
  • 5. Never go to someone’s room, apartment or house that you just met.

  • 1. When leaving your dorm room, home, or office, lock doors and windows even if you will be gone for "just a minute."
  • 2. Never leave your purse, wallet, or valuables exposed; store them out of sight. Be especially careful with your credit cards, which are very popular items among thieves because they are usually easy to steal and then use again. Consider obtaining a credit card with your photo imprinted on it.
  • 3. Use a nickname in chat rooms or message boards.
  • 4. Meet chat friends in public places and with other friends; take a cell phone with you.
  • 5. Keep a list of all items and serial numbers in a safe place.
